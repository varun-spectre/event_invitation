It's Not About Blame

I want to start by saying — this isn't about pointing fingers or making you feel bad. This is about us, about making things work, and about being real with each other.

Relationships are a two-way street. Both people need to show up, put in the effort, and think about the other person. Lately, I feel like that balance has been off.

What I've Been Feeling

There have been moments where I felt like my feelings, my time, and my efforts didn't matter as much. Here are some things I've noticed:

  • When I make plans around your schedule but my plans get dismissed easily.
  • When I go out of my way to do something thoughtful and it goes unacknowledged.
  • When conversations always circle back to what you need, without asking how I'm doing.
  • When compromises only happen on my side and rarely on yours.
  • When I express something that bothers me and it gets brushed off or turned around.

What I Need From You

I'm not asking for perfection. I'm asking for awareness. I need you to:

Pause and think — "Am I considering his feelings too, or just my own right now?"

That one question, asked honestly, can change everything. I want to be partners — not someone who just goes along with everything to keep the peace.

I need reciprocity. I need to feel valued. I need to know that when I show up for you, you're willing to show up for me too.

Because I Love You

I'm writing this because I care. If I didn't, I wouldn't bother. I'd just walk away. But I don't want to walk away — I want us to grow together.

I believe we can be better. But it takes both of us recognizing where we fall short and choosing to do better. That's all I'm asking.

Let's Fix This Together

This isn't the end of anything. It's the start of a more honest chapter. I love you — and that's exactly why I'm saying all of this.
